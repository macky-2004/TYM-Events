Behind the Scenes

The "almost disaster" stories

We share stories of things that almost went wrong and how our systems caught them. Competence without arrogance.

The Priest Who Had Two Weddings Booked at the Same Muhurtham

Two days before a Tamil wedding in Coimbatore, our vendor management system flagged that the lead priest had double-booked himself for the same Muhurtham window. Our daily standup caught it during the morning review. Within 3 hours, we had a highly recommended backup priest confirmed, the original priest briefed the replacement on the family's specific rituals, and the family was informed — coolly and calmly. The bride's father said: "We didn't even know there was a problem." That is exactly the point.

The Caterer Who Underestimated for a Christian Wedding

A caterer for a 300-person Christian wedding reception in Madurai confirmed they could handle the guest count — but our budget analysis flagged that their quantity commitment was below market standard for that menu. We investigated and found they had misjudged the serving portions. We negotiated an upgrade at no extra cost to the family and confirmed with the caterer's backup kitchen. The reception was flawless. The family never knew there was a risk.
