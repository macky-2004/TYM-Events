# Section 1: Brand Foundation & Business Identity

### Q1. What inspired you to start this business?
I attended hundreds of events — weddings, corporate galas, brand launches — and every single one had a broken experience. Beautiful venue, terrible coordination. Amazing catering, confusing timelines. The logistics were always invisible to guests, but as an organizer I saw the chaos behind the curtain. I realized there was no single platform that combined premium aesthetics with enterprise-grade operational control. I wanted to build the operating system for unforgettable events.

### Q2. What problem were you trying to solve when you started?
Event management today is fragmented. Clients use spreadsheets, WhatsApp groups, and sticky notes. Vendors operate in silos. Guests have no real-time visibility. The problem is not a lack of tools — it's a lack of an integrated, premium experience that serves all three stakeholders (client, vendor, guest) on one unified platform. We set out to eliminate the chaos and replace it with clarity, elegance, and control.

### Q3. What does your brand stand for emotionally?
Certainty. When you book TYM Events, you feel a weight lift off your shoulders. You stop worrying and start enjoying. Our brand stands for the peace of mind that comes from knowing every detail is handled by someone who cares as much as you do.

### Q4. What values are most important in your business?
- **Obsessive attention to detail** — we notice what others overlook
- **Radical transparency** — real-time dashboards, no hidden surprises
- **White-glove service** — every client is treated like a VIP
- **Execution excellence** — we deliver what we promise, on time, every time
- **Continuous innovation** — we invest in technology that makes events better

### Q5. What makes your business different from others in the same field?
Most event companies are either tech-first (ugly, functional) or service-first (beautiful, chaotic). We are both. Our platform has a luxury UI/UX that rivals a high-end consumer app, combined with operational depth that a professional planner needs. We don't just plan events — we engineer experiences with data, design, and discipline.

### Q6. What is the biggest strength of your business?
Our end-to-end visibility. A client can open a dashboard and see vendor status, budget burn, guest RSVPs, timeline adherence, and real-time issues — all in one place. No other event company gives this level of transparency with this level of polish.

### Q7. What do customers remember most about your brand?
That everything just worked. They didn't have to chase anyone. They didn't have to remind anyone. They showed up, enjoyed, and left feeling like royalty. The absence of stress is what they remember most.

### Q8. What is the "wow factor" of your business?
The live event dashboard. During the event, guests and the client can see a real-time timeline, photo stream, announcements, and interactive floor plans on their phones — all branded to match the event theme. It's like having a dedicated event app without installing anything.

### Q9. If your brand had a personality, how would you describe it?
Sophisticated but warm. A calm, capable professional who has everything under control but never makes you feel small. Think of a world-class concierge at a five-star hotel — they anticipate your needs before you articulate them.

### Q10. What kind of experience do you want customers to feel after interacting with your business?
Relief, then delight. Relief that the complexity was absorbed by us. Delight that the result exceeded their imagination. We want them to feel like they discovered a secret that everyone else is missing.

# Section 2: Customer Understanding

### Q11. Who are your ideal customers?
High-net-worth individuals, corporate event managers at Fortune 500 companies, wedding couples with budgets above $50,000, luxury brand marketing teams, and destination event organizers who need a trusted remote partner.

### Q12. What age group mostly buys from you?
30–55. The sweet spot is 35–45 — established in their careers, experienced enough to know what quality looks like, busy enough to need a reliable partner.

### Q13. What type of customers usually return again and again?
Corporate clients who host recurring events (annual galas, product launches, team offsites) and wedding planners who refer us to their premium clients. Repeat business comes from those who value reliability over cost savings.

### Q14. What emotions drive customers to choose your business?
Anxiety and aspiration. They are anxious about something going wrong on a high-stakes day. They aspire to impress their guests, their boss, their family. We convert that anxiety into confidence and that aspiration into reality.

### Q15. What common problems do customers face before coming to you?
Scope creep (vendors adding hidden costs), communication breakdowns between stakeholders, timeline slippage, lack of a single source of truth, and the exhausting mental load of coordinating dozens of moving parts alone.

### Q16. What do customers appreciate most about your service?
Single-point accountability. They have one person (and one platform) that owns the entire outcome. No more "that's the caterer's problem" or "the venue handles that." We own it all.

### Q17. What do customers usually ask before purchasing?
"Can you really handle everything?" and "What happens if something goes wrong on the day?" These questions reveal their deepest fear: loss of control. Our answer is the live dashboard, the backup plans baked into every timeline, and our 15-minute response time SLA.

### Q18. What builds trust fastest with your customers?
A detailed, visual walkthrough of a past event similar to theirs. When they see the timeline, the vendor coordination record, the budget tracking, and the post-event analytics, they realize this is not a hobby — it's a professionally engineered system.

### Q19. What kind of customers are hardest to satisfy?
Control enthusiasts who want to micromanage every detail. They are hard to satisfy because they don't fully delegate. Our approach is to give them a "view-only" dashboard that satisfies their need for visibility without letting them disrupt the operational workflow.

### Q20. What customer behavior do you notice most often?
They show the dashboard to their friends. The platform becomes a status symbol — "Look at how organized my event is." This organic sharing is our best marketing channel.

We started as a traditional event planning company. We built the software internally because we couldn't find tools that met our standards. The realization that we are not in the event business — we are in the trust business. Events are just the medium. Trust is the product. Every decision should be filtered through: "Does this build or erode trust?"

Referrals. Not because we asked for them, but because the platform naturally created advocates. Clients showed the dashboard to their peers, and the peers wanted it too. Product-led growth in a service business.

### Q36. What challenges are you currently facing?
Scaling the service layer without diluting quality. Our full-service offering is inherently people-intensive, and finding people who share our obsessive standards is hard. We are investing heavily in training systems and AI-assisted workflows.

### Q37. What are your future expansion plans?
- Geographic expansion to top 10 global event destinations
- A marketplace connecting premium vendors with clients
- AI-powered event design assistant
- White-label platform for hotel chains and venues

### Q38. Where do you see your business in the next 5 years?
The definitive operating system for premium events globally. If a high-stakes event is happening anywhere in the world, TYM Events should be either running it or powering it.

### Q39. What kind of legacy do you want your business to create?
We want to redefine what people expect from event management. Just as Uber changed what people expect from transportation, we want to change what people expect from events — that they can be stress-free, beautiful, and predictable all at once.

### Q40. What motivates you to continue this business journey?
The messages we get at 2 AM after an event: "I've never felt so taken care of. Thank you." That feeling of being someone's safety net, of delivering a memory that families will talk about for decades — that is the fuel.

(See above) — but critically, every step is tracked in the platform with checklists, approvals, and status updates visible to the client.

### Q74. What internal process makes your service better than others?
The daily standup — every morning at 9 AM, the operations team reviews every active event's status, flags risks, and assigns actions. No event goes more than 24 hours without a structured review.

### Q75. Which tasks consume the most time daily?
Communication. Answering client questions, coordinating vendor updates, managing changes. We are building AI-powered chatbots and automated status updates to reduce this burden by 40%.

### Q76. What operational challenges slow down growth?
Finding and training talent that meets our standards. Our service quality is directly proportional to our people quality. We are investing in a TYM Academy to systematize training and reduce dependence on hiring experienced talent.

### Q77. Which areas do you want to improve in efficiency?
Vendor payment processing — currently too manual. Also, document management (contracts, invoices, permits) is still not fully digitized. We want a paperless, automated back office.

### Q78. What communication methods do customers prefer?
WhatsApp for quick updates, email for formal communications, and the in-platform messaging for everything related to the event dashboard. We meet customers where they are.

### Q79. Which process creates the best customer satisfaction?
The pre-event walkthrough. Two days before the event, we do a full simulation with the client — timeline, contingency plans, vendor confirmations, and a "what if" session. Clients consistently rate this as the most reassuring part of the journey.

### Q80. What part of the business requires better organization?

# PRD: Technical & Functional Requirements

## 1. Platform Overview

**Product Name:** TYM Events Platform  
**Product Type:** SaaS + Service Marketplace  
**Target Users:** Event Clients, Event Planners (internal), Vendors, Guests  
**Platform:** Web-first (PWA), Native iOS/Android (Phase 2)

## 2. User Roles & Permissions

| Role | Permissions |
|------|------------|
| **Client (Admin)** | Full visibility: budget, timeline, vendors, guest list, RSVPs, task status, post-event analytics. Can approve/reject milestones, communicate with planner, view live event feed. |
| **Client (View-Only)** | Read-only access to dashboard. Cannot make changes. Ideal for partners, family members, or executives who want visibility without control. |
| **Planner (Internal)** | Full operational access: manage vendors, update timelines, track budget, assign tasks, communicate with all stakeholders, generate reports. |
| **Vendor** | Limited to their scope: view their tasks, upload deliverables, confirm milestones, communicate with planner, submit invoices. Cannot see other vendors' details or client budget. |
| **Guest** | Event-specific micro-site: view event details, RSVP, upload photos, see live timeline, interact with event feed. No login required (access via unique URL). |
| **Super Admin** | Cross-event visibility, platform configuration, user management, billing, analytics dashboard for business KPIs. |

## 3. Core Modules

### 3.1 Event Dashboard
- Real-time timeline with drag-and-drop rescheduling
- Budget tracker with actual vs. planned, burn rate, alerts
- Vendor management: contact, contracts, status, deliverables
- Guest management: RSVP tracking, dietary restrictions, seating
- Task management: checklist with assignees, due dates, dependencies
- Notification center: all communications in one thread per event

### 3.2 Client Portal
- Welcome kit with branded assets
- Milestone approval workflow
- Document vault (contracts, invoices, permits)
- Messaging center with planner (WhatsApp bridge)
- Live event feed (during event)

### 3.3 Vendor Portal
- Profile management with portfolio
- Task dashboard with deadlines
- Document upload (invoices, contracts, deliverables)
- Two-way communication with planner
- Payment tracking

### 3.4 Guest Experience
- Event micro-site with custom branding
- RSVP and preference collection
- Live schedule and announcements
- Photo sharing and gallery
- Interactive seating map
- Push notifications (if app) or SMS/email

### 3.5 Operations Backend
- Multi-event management for planners
- Time tracking and resource allocation
- SOP checklist per event type
- Risk register and contingency planner
- Daily standup integration

### 3.6 Post-Event Module
- Analytics dashboard: attendance rate, engagement score, timeline adherence, budget accuracy
- Photo/video gallery delivery
- Feedback collection and NPS survey
- Client archive with search

## 4. Technical Requirements

### 4.1 Performance
- Page load time: < 2 seconds (P95)
- Real-time updates: < 500ms latency for dashboard updates
- Support for 10,000 concurrent guests per event
- 99.9% uptime SLA

### 4.2 Security
- SOC 2 compliance
- End-to-end encryption for all communications
- Role-based access control with audit logging
- GDPR and CCPA compliant
- 2FA for all internal accounts
- Encrypted document storage (AES-256)

### 4.3 Integrations
- Payment gateways: Stripe, Razorpay
- Calendar: Google Calendar, Outlook, iCal
- Communication: WhatsApp API, Twilio (SMS), SendGrid (email)
- Storage: AWS S3 / CloudFront for media
- CRM: HubSpot, Salesforce (Phase 2)
- Accounting: QuickBooks, Xero (Phase 2)

### 4.4 Technology Stack (Recommended)
- **Frontend:** Next.js (React) with TypeScript, TailwindCSS
- **Backend:** Node.js (NestJS) or Python (FastAPI)
- **Database:** PostgreSQL (primary), Redis (caching, real-time)
- **Real-time:** WebSocket (Socket.io or AWS AppSync)
- **Mobile:** React Native (Phase 2)
- **Infrastructure:** AWS (ECS, RDS, CloudFront, S3)
- **CI/CD:** GitHub Actions + Docker
